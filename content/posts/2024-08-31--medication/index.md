Obviously, it's been a while since I've posted.

At some point I decided that, since I'm getting medicated for high blood pressure, rheumatoid arthritis, and a couple other things, I might as well get medicated for the thing that's actually caused me more suffering than anything else: my ADHD.

So I met with a friendly local nurse practitioner, he diagnosed me, and boom, I was prescribed. Quick, easy, painless. Well, I was prescribed Vyvanse, but apparently there's a national shortage of that, so I went on Concerta instead. Vyvanse would supposedly help with anxiety too. Concerta may not, but we'll get to that.

How's it been? Well, my quick capsule review: _wheeeeeeeeeeeeee_.

It's been up and down, but mostly up. I've found that I'm definitely concentrating better. As was the case with my child when he went on stimulant medication, I feel like I actually _matured_ significantly within the first couple of months of using it. It seemed like I was able to put the little irritating details of everyday life better into perspective. I felt like I had better self-control. I also felt substantial improvements in my overall executive function, like, I finally got outside and weeded the flowerbeds, and now that they are a sterile, lifeless wasteland I feel much better.

I think – and this is weird – that it's made me more assertive. Which is not entirely a good thing, because I am often an absolute prick and only my fear of making a mistake and looking like an idiot will keep me from being rude or insubordinate. My filter has definitely relaxed a bit, but perhaps it's time that it did so.

I've been staggeringly productive at work. I've almost always been _good_, in that I would consistently work my eight hours with focus and mindfulness. But lately I've been working like a maniac, which is good because I have a _ton_ of work to do migrating our products from one AWS account to another and rewriting all of the IaC and adding features and fixing bugs as I go. I have a bright shiny new AWS account and am writing all of the IaC from scratch, so I feel like a bit like a kid in a candy store.

Therein lies the rub – a bit - because it's hard to fight the temptation to work late, work weekends, etc. I have, a bit, and that concerns me. I feel like if I work too many hours in too short a time, I'll burn myself out. At the same time, I'm _really_ enjoying what I'm doing, I'm learning a tremendous amount, and I'm improving our products. I don't _feel_ like I'm suffering any adverse affects from this. I don't particularly want to do anything else.

So I feel something kind of like a reverse Puritan work ethic, where I feel a bit weird about how much intrinsic motivation I have. I've always felt a sort of restlessness as an adult, a difficulty in relaxing and enjoying myself when I felt I could be doing something "productive," but that never came to much. What tended to happen was that I wouldn't do anything fun, but I wouldn't actually get anything important done either. Now I feel like that restlessness is just focused into action.

For the most part, I'm sleeping well, although sometimes if I wake up in the middle of the night, my thoughts will keep me from getting back to sleep easily. I'm not sure if that's a rebound effect or something else.

Other than that, life's going quite well. I'm exercising regularly (just hit a 300-lb squat yesterday, and I'm still biking and rowing six days a week) and eating healthily for the most part (these Mike & Ike Mega Mix Sours are seriously difficult to resist; I bought about thirty pounds from Menard's recently).
