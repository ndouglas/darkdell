# Continuity

> The reason for which humans have failed to develop a finely built social process assuring continuity and steady quality in leadership is probably that they did not have to. Most human societies are marked by the existence of a surplus beyond subsistence. The counterpart of this surplus is society's ability to take considerable deterioration in its stride. A lower level of performance, which would mean disaster for baboons, merely causes discomfort, at least initially, to humans.
>
> - Albert O. Hirschman, _Exit, Voice, and Loyalty: Responses to Decline in Firms, Organizations, and States_
