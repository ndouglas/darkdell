Honeyholt is a Rust library that provides a highly flexible artificial intelligence engine for non-player characters (NPCs) in video games. The library is designed to be highly modular and plugin-based, so that developers can choose from a wide range of techniques and approaches for creating intelligent NPCs.

<!--more-->

## Summary

Honeyholt is an artificial intelligence engine designed to provide flexible and modular NPC behaviors in video games. The library allows developers to choose from a variety of AI techniques, including human-written scripts, behavior trees, goal-oriented action planning, and more. Honeyholt also includes tools for building and managing complex, multi-layered NPC behaviors, so that developers can create characters that respond dynamically to changes in the game world and player actions.

The library is built using Rust, and is based on the latest techniques and algorithms from the field of artificial intelligence. It is designed to be highly extensible and customizable, so that developers can easily add their own plugins and features to the system.

### Naming

Honeyholt is a castle in the Reach that is associated with bees and honey, and the name reflects the idea of creating intelligent NPCs that are buzzing with activity, like a beehive. The name also evokes the idea of a modular system, where individual components work together in a coordinated way to achieve a common goal.

## Motivations

The motivation for this project comes from a desire to create more realistic and engaging NPCs in video games. Traditional NPC behavior is often limited and predictable, and can quickly become repetitive and boring for players. The goal of Honeyholt is to provide developers with a tool for creating intelligent, dynamic, and engaging NPCs that can respond in real-time to changes in the game world and player actions.

## Challenges

One of the main challenges of this project is the sheer complexity of creating flexible and modular AI systems. There are many different techniques and approaches for creating intelligent behavior in NPCs, and each has its own strengths and weaknesses. To tackle this challenge, we plan to build a flexible plugin-based system that allows developers to choose from a range of different techniques and algorithms. We will also be consulting with experts in the field of artificial intelligence to ensure that our models and algorithms are as accurate and up-to-date as possible.
