Pennytree is a Rust library designed for generating unique languages with rich phonetic and grammatical structures. It includes a powerful engine for interpreting phonemes and generating grammatical rules, allowing you to create languages with a wide range of influences and unique characteristics.

## Summary

Pennytree is a Rust library that lets you procedurally generate languages with unique phonetic and grammatical structures. It's built on top of Rust, which makes it fast and efficient, and the language engine is designed to be highly versatile and customizable, allowing you to create languages with a wide range of influences and unique characteristics.

The library is built with a focus on natural language processing and artificial intelligence, making it an ideal choice for game developers looking to create unique and immersive worlds with believable and complex languages.

### Naming

Pennytree is a small village in the Riverlands, and the name is reminiscent of the idea of a "tree of languages" or a "family tree" of languages. This name fits well with the goal of creating a library that allows for the generation of unique and diverse languages.

## Motivations

The motivation for this project comes from a deep interest in linguistics and artificial intelligence, as well as a fascination with the idea of creating complex and realistic virtual worlds. The goal of Pennytree is to provide a tool that makes it easier for other developers to explore these topics and create their own virtual worlds that are scientifically accurate and richly detailed.

## Features

- A powerful engine for generating unique phonetic and grammatical structures
- Support for a wide range of influences and characteristics, allowing you to create unique languages with rich cultural backgrounds
- A focus on natural language processing and artificial intelligence
- Built on top of Rust, making it fast and efficient

## Challenges

One of the main challenges of this project is creating a language engine that is both versatile and easy to use. It can be difficult to balance the need for flexibility with the need for simplicity, but we believe that we've struck a good balance with Pennytree.

Another challenge is creating a library that is powerful and versatile enough to support a wide range of influences and characteristics, while also being easy to integrate into existing projects.
